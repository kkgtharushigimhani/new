Week 1

Literature Review

Research Articles Reviewed

Two key journal papers were reviewed this week, forming the scientific foundation for the IWMTAS project. Both focus on temperature-based wound monitoring and thermal therapy in wound healing.

Research Article Review — Paper 1

Clinical Significance of Wound Temperature Monitoring in Diabetic Foot Microcirculation Reconstruction

Hong Liu, Xian-Yan Yan, Guo-Qing Li, Bao-Na Wang, Dong Wang, Yong-Hong Zhang, Jin-Li Guo

Background

Diabetic Foot Ulcers (DFUs) arise from chronic hyperglycemia causing vascular endothelial damage, micro-thrombosis, and microcirculation failure. Individuals with diabetes face a 25% lifetime risk of developing a foot ulcer, with 50–60% progressing to chronic non-healing wounds.

Method Used

Retrospective study of 29 patients (median age 57.14 ± 14.75 yrs, Wagner grade ≥ 3). Infrared skin temperature was recorded at the wound center and distal tibia (5 cm above ankle), at admission, weekly for 1 month, then monthly for 3 months. Room temp: 20–25°C.

Treatment

Transverse Tibial Bone Transport (distraction histogenesis) was used to stimulate neurovascular regeneration and collateral microvessel formation. Ultrasound debridement was used to clear biofilms before readings. Silver-containing lipid hydrocolloid dressings maintained the wound environment.

Key Findings
33°C Biological Threshold
Below this level, neutrophil, fibroblast, and keratinocyte activity drops sharply — risk of total healing failure.
Equilibrium Point (Week 4)
P-value rises to 0.283 — temperature convergence between wound center and distal tibia = healing success marker.
SMI Validation
Superb Microvascular Imaging confirmed temperature rise directly correlates with increased microvascular density.
93% Limb Salvage Rate
27 of 29 patients achieved wound repair — zero re-amputations for those completing the full protocol.
WIFi Grade Improvement
Wound grade: 3→0, Ischemia: 1→0, Infection: 3→0. All improvements statistically significant (P<0.05).
Case Highlight
64-year-old male with 13-year diabetes history achieved full wound closure in 4.5 months via bone transport.
Relevance to IWMTAS: This paper directly underpins our system's core function — using skin temperature as a real-time, non-invasive proxy for microcirculation status. The 33°C threshold and Equilibrium Point concept will be programmed as key alert triggers in our intelligent monitoring system.
Technical Report Review — Paper 2

Topical Radiant Heating (TRH) in Wound Healing

Experimental study using donor site wound model — Warm-Up™ Dressing System & Laser Doppler Imaging

What is TRH?

Topical Radiant Heating delivers constant radiant heat at 38°C via the Warm-Up™ dressing system (Arizant Healthcare). It maintains a humid environment to facilitate re-epithelialization and collagen synthesis, powered by AC or portable battery.

Study Design

Study 1: 20 healthy volunteers — intermittent TRH on right thigh, left as control. Study 2: 12 patients with split-thickness skin grafts — 6 received TRH, 6 standard dressings. Protocol: 3× 1-hour heating cycles over 5 hours.

Measurement Tools

Laser Doppler Imaging (LDI) using helium-neon laser (632.8 nm) mapped dermal microvascular blood flow. Immunohistochemical analysis tracked NP57 (neutrophils), CD3 (T-lymphocytes), and CD68 (macrophages) via Streptavidin-Biotin staining.

Van't Hoff's Law in Action
Applied heat accelerates biochemical reactions — hyperthermia stimulates fibroblast proliferation and enhances polymorph chemotaxis and phagocytosis.
Oxygen Tension Elevation
TRH increases local dermal blood flow and subcutaneous oxygen tension, enabling oxygen-dependent collagen cross-linking via prolyl 4-hydroxylase.
Immune Regulatory Effect
TRH enhances innate immunity — early lymphocytic infiltrate regulates neutrophil activity, preventing chaotic cytokine release for superior healing prognosis.
Temporal Blood Flow
Blood flow returns to near-baseline within 1 hour of TRH cessation, while subcutaneous oxygen tension may remain elevated for up to 3 hours post-treatment.
Relevance to IWMTAS: TRH will be the primary therapy module in our system. The 38°C target temperature, 1-hour cycle duration, and LDI-validated blood flow response form the basis of our adaptive therapy scheduling algorithm. The 3-hour post-treatment oxygen elevation window will also be incorporated into therapy timing logic.
